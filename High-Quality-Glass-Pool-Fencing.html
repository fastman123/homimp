Barriers
All swimming pools must be included by an approved barrier. The barrier has to meet state authorities pool security legislation under chapter 8 of their Building Act 1975. A barrier can comprise:

Fencing to your pool
walls of a building surrounding the pool
another kind of barrier mentioned or provided for at the pool security standard.
Approved barriers are just one portion of swimming pool security.

Your duties
Property owners who have pools are responsible for ensuring compliant pool obstacles are in place at all times.

Tenants also have duties, such as:

Maintaining pool gates shut
ensuring there aren't any objects that would permit children to access the pool unattended.
All pool owners must register their pool around the statewide pool register.

If you sell or rent your property, you have to receive a pool security certificate.

Pool safety standard
The present pool safety standard was introduced on 1 December 2010. All pools, new and existing, must comply with the standard.

Refer to the Department of Housing and Public Works website for More Information.

Pool safety certificates
A pool safety certification, issued by a certified pool safety inspector, is needed when selling, leasing or buying a home with a pool. Pool safety certificates are valid for one year to get a shared pool and 2 years to get a non-shared pool.

Leasing a property
Your landlord or body corporate must obtain a pool safety certification before a lease is signed. The existing certificate covers any new leases or renewals that occur in that interval.

Purchasing a property
If There Is Absolutely No pool security certificate in effect before entering a contract or earlier settlement, then the seller should provide an advisory notice using a note of no pool safety certification to this:

The Queensland Government has an internet register of accredited pool safety inspectors. All inspectors hold a licence suggesting they are licensed to carry out inspections. Exemptions
You are able to lodge an application with Council to get an exemption. It's important to note that Council will consider the security of young kids before committing any exemptions.

Disability exemption
The exemption application should comprise:

The form and degree of the disability
whether the disabled individual is wheelchair-bound or cellular
if wheelchair-bound, whether the wheelchair is able to move unaided
whether the disabled individual requires a fulltime carer
medical evidence to support the application
a description of which provisions of the barrier regulations forbid the disabled person from gaining entry to, and exiting from, the pool enclosure
a description of the replacement preventative measures which will prevent young children gaining access into the pool.
