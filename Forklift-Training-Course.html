The EU Occupational Safety Health Administration requires all forklift operators be accredited prior to running a lift truck or warehouse item. Proper training and assessment is completed prior to the operation of industrial forklifts. The objective of a forklift certification is to reduce the number of injuries due to hazardous lift truck usage in the workplace.

Training must be finished by individuals who have understanding, training and experience to train forklift operators. This makes sure that operators are trained and accredited to deal with the complexities of their particular work environment. After training is finished, there should be an examination of operator efficiency in the work environment.

A forklift license is actually a certificate. The certificate indicates that the staff member has actually been trained and assessed by the company to run the truck safely. It is required by EU-OSHA that the company accredits the training. License issuance is used by employers to show the employee is certified to operate a forklift in their specific application.
