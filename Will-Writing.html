Lasting Power Of Attorney

So, What is Lasting Power Of Attorney?

While drawing up a will is an important thing to do in the context of estate planning, creating a Lasting Power of Attorney can also be equally so. That’s because it enables one or more people to be nominated as being able to make key decisions about your financial and personal affairs. It will add to the cost of making a will, but it’s certainly worth it.

In the event that you lose your physical and mental capacity to decide things for yourself, this important legal document allows someone you trust to be named ‘executor of estate’. It’s also something that allows your next of kin to legally decide how your estate is distributed upon your passing.

Within the document itself, you can place legally-binding instructions relating to your general wishes that are used to guide future decisions about dependants, assets, wills and trusts as part of the probate service. It’s only possible to create a Lasting Power of Attorney when you have the mental capacity to understand what it is you’re signing - that unless a Deputyship Order is applied for by your next of kin.


There are two main areas of your affairs that are governed by a Lasting Power Attorney.

1.Health and Welfare - which relates to decisions around the type of care you require, who it is provided by and where it is provided. However, perhaps the most important decision they might ultimately have to make is whether to continue life-sustaining treatment if and when the time came.

2.Property & Financial - which allows your nominated person to deal with paying bills, matters relating to trust law, managing your property assets and making investment decisions.

All of the key decisions relating to the most important aspects of your life - that you would normally make for yourself - will be taken care of by the person who has your lasting power of attorney and it is typically a job left to a close family member.
The Benefits of Creating a Lasting Power of Attorney

Lasting Power Of Attorney More Information

There are a number of advantages to appointing a close family member or next of kin to deal with your affairs when you are unable to:

●It’s an affordable way to take care of what can be a complex arrangement
●It gives you the peace of mind that the best decisions are made regarding your personal welfare and finances
●It leaves these decisions in the hands of your next of kin and uninfluenced by local authorities

Even after Lasting Power of Attorney has been signed and registered with the Court of Protection, you will still be able to make your own decisions. It’s only when you agree that your attorney(s) should be allowed to intervene that decisions will then be made on your behalf.
What Would Happen to My Property Trust and Assets Without a Lasting Power of Attorney?

The wisdom of having a Last Power of Attorney would become all-too apparent to your relatives if you lost your mental capacity to take care of your affairs and didn’t have one in place. The alternative is to get the courts to appoint a person to be your deputy attorney, however, this has been known to take up to a year to be put into place.

Meanwhile, your loved ones may have their assets frozen the whole time and this is likely to lead to the kind of financial hardship that you just wouldn’t want your loved ones to go through. Not only that, but the powers of a deputy attorney are quite limited and using a court-appointed probate lawyer can be very expensive - which is the last thing they need if they are already finding it difficult to make ends meet.

No one would knowingly put their loved ones through this kind of trauma and setting up a Lasting Power of Attorney is very simple in comparison to the alternative. It’s a very sensible move for anyone looking to secure the financial and emotional future of those close to them.
